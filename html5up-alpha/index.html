Irish people are wealthier than ever before, as the average Irish household net worth reaches over €150,000, the highest in the history of the state. The average Irish household has reached a net worth of €158,574 with the national total at a record high of €780.4 billion in quarter 2, 2019 according to new data from the Central Bank.

Household net worth accounts for the assets minus the debts of each home. At the peak of the Celtic Tiger, household net worth was valued at €719 billion. This has been surpassed every quarter since the beginning of 2018. The latest figures have risen by 1.6 per cent, driven by improvements in both financial and housing assets. The increases were largely down to an increase in financial assets which rose by €5 billion, due primarily to increases in holdings of deposits and insurance technical reserves.

Housing assets rose to €537 billion, an increase of €5.6 billion over the quarter, the highest it has been since Q4 2008. The rising value of housing likely has contributed to this increase in assets, yet housing debts have also fallen. Household liabilities fell by €1.6 billion during the quarter, falling to their lowest level since 2005. This averages €27,489 per capita, largely due to decreases in long-term loans. While the data offers a cumulative glance at the increased prosperity currently being experienced by the economy, it does not reveal how that prosperity is being distributed across different income or age groups across Irish society.
